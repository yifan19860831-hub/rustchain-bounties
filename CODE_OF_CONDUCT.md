# RustChain Code of Conduct

## 🦀 Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in the **RustChain community** a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an **open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community** that embodies the RustChain values of innovation, authenticity, and mutual respect.

---

## 🎯 Our Values

### 1. **Innovation Through Diversity**
We believe that diverse perspectives drive better solutions. Whether you're mining with vintage hardware or building the next generation of decentralized applications, your unique background strengthens our community.

### 2. **Authentic Participation**
RustChain is built on the principle of **Proof-of-Antiquity** — real hardware, real contributions, real people (and their agents). We value genuine engagement over superficial metrics.

### 3. **Mutual Respect**
Every contributor matters, from first-time participants to long-time maintainers. We treat all community members with dignity and respect.

### 4. **Transparency**
We operate openly. Decisions, discussions, and development happen in public whenever possible. Trust is earned through visibility and consistency.

### 5. **Collaborative Growth**
We succeed together. Whether you're onboarding new miners, reviewing PRs, or sharing knowledge, your contributions help the entire ecosystem thrive.

---

## ✅ Our Standards

### Positive Behavior

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- **Demonstrating empathy and kindness** toward other people
- **Being respectful** of differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences
- **Giving and gracefully accepting** constructive feedback
- **Accepting responsibility** and apologizing to those affected by our mistakes, and learning from the experience
- **Focusing on what is best** for the overall community, not just individuals
- **Welcoming newcomers** and helping them navigate the project
- **Sharing knowledge** freely to help others grow
- **Respecting the work** of others, including proper attribution
- **Being patient** with questions, especially from those new to blockchain or Rust

### Unacceptable Behavior

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **The use of sexualized language or imagery**, and sexual attention or advances of any kind
- **Trolling, insulting or derogatory comments**, and personal or political attacks
- **Public or private harassment**
- **Publishing others' private information** (doxxing), such as physical or email addresses, without explicit permission
- **Spamming or disruptive promotion** unrelated to RustChain
- **Manipulative behavior** including astroturfing, fake engagement, or bounty fraud
- **Other conduct** which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

---

## 🔍 Scope

This Code of Conduct applies:

- **Within all RustChain community spaces** (GitHub, Discord, Twitter/X, forums, events)
- **When representing RustChain publicly** (official social media, conferences, meetups)
- **To all participants** (contributors, maintainers, miners, users, agents)
- **To both human and agent participants** (AI agents must follow the same standards as their operators)

## 🛡️ Enforcement Responsibilities

### Community Leaders

Community leaders (maintainers, moderators, designated enforcers) are responsible for:

- **Clarifying standards** of acceptable behavior
- **Enforcing standards** fairly and consistently
- **Taking appropriate corrective action** in response to violations
- **Removing, editing, or rejecting** contributions not aligned with this Code
- **Communicating decisions** with transparency (while respecting privacy)

### Reporting Violations

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported to:

- **Email:** scott@elyanlabs.ai
- **GitHub:** @Scottcjn
- **Discord:** Community Moderators

**All complaints will be reviewed promptly and fairly.**

### Privacy & Protection

- Reporters' identities are kept **confidential**
- Retaliation against reporters is **strictly prohibited**
- All parties are treated with **respect and dignity** throughout the process

## ⚖️ Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders follow these **Community Impact Guidelines** when determining consequences:

### Level 1: Correction

**Impact:** Minor violation (inappropriate language, unintentional offense)

**Consequence:**
- Private written warning
- Explanation of why behavior was inappropriate
- Optional public apology request

**Goal:** Education and correction

---

### Level 2: Warning

**Impact:** Single incident or pattern of minor violations

**Consequence:**
- Formal written warning
- Temporary no-contact order with involved parties
- Possible temporary restriction from community spaces

**Duration:** Typically 1-4 weeks

**Goal:** Behavior change with clear consequences

---

### Level 3: Temporary Ban

**Impact:** Serious violation or continued pattern after warnings

**Consequence:**
- Temporary ban from all community interaction
- No public or private communication with community members
- Loss of contributor privileges

**Duration:** Typically 1-6 months

**Goal:** Protect community while allowing for rehabilitation

---

### Level 4: Permanent Ban

**Impact:** Severe violation, sustained harassment, or pattern of abuse

**Consequence:**
- Permanent ban from all community spaces
- Removal from contributor lists
- No future participation allowed

**Goal:** Protect community from irredeemable bad actors

---

## 📋 Enforcement Process

### 1. Report Received
- Complaint logged and acknowledged within **48 hours**
- Initial assessment of severity

### 2. Investigation
- Relevant evidence collected
- All parties interviewed (if appropriate)
- Timeline: **5-10 business days**

### 3. Decision
- Community leaders review findings
- Appropriate consequence determined
- Decision documented

### 4. Action
- Consequence implemented
- Parties notified
- Public statement (if warranted, anonymized)

### 5. Appeal (Optional)
- Appeals accepted within **14 days**
- Reviewed by different community leader
- Final decision within **10 business days**
